Satoru Iwata

Gaming pioneer and former Nintendo CEO

Beginnings

Satoru Iwata began his programming journey with an HP-65 calculator, the first of its kind with programmable memory. Using this, he developed several games for his highschool friends to play. in 1978 he got his first computer, a Commodore PET which he dismantled to get a better understanding of.

HAL Laboratories

Iwata became a full time employee at HAL Laboratory, a videogame development company in 1980. After being integral to the development of many successful games (such as Kirby and Earthbound) Iwata was promoted to president of HAL in 1993. At this time, he worked closely with Nintendo, providing instrumental support on several titles in the Pok√©mon franchise.

Nintendo

Iwata joined Nintendo in 2000, subsequently becoming company President in 2002. Iwata's focus was on being able to expand gaming to a new audience, with concern sales were declining due to a focus on the established customer base. This approach to making consoles with broader appeal resulted in the Nintendo DS and Wii consoles, which went on to become some of the most sucessful selling consoles of all time. Iwata continued his role at Nintendo and oversaw the development of the Nintendo Switch, until he sadly passed away from cancer at the age of 55.

This site contains a series of interviews Iwata conducted with staff from Nintendo, which gives insight into the game development process.

"On my business card, I am a corporate president. In my mind, I am a game developer. But in my heart, I am a gamer." - Satoru Iwata.
